What It Is

Not just coping — genuine cognitive transformation.

Cognitive therapy at the Insight Center is not symptom management. It is a sustained inquiry into the structure of your own thinking — the patterns, assumptions, and mental habits that shape how you experience yourself and the world.

Grounded in Bernard Lonergan's philosophy of intentional consciousness and Reuven Feuerstein's theory of Mediated Learning Experience, our approach treats cognition as something that can be observed, understood, and fundamentally changed.

This is not a quick fix. It is a rigorous, rewarding process of developing the kind of self-knowledge that sustains lasting change.

"The goal is not to feel better about how you think — it is to think better. The feeling follows."

The Process

What working with us actually looks like.

Every client's path is different, but the process follows a consistent structure designed to build genuine insight at each stage.

1
Initial Consultation

A free 30-minute conversation to understand your situation and determine whether our approach is a good fit. No commitment required.

2
Cognitive Assessment

A structured assessment process that maps your current cognitive functioning — strengths, gaps, and the specific areas where development will be most impactful.

3
Individual Sessions

Weekly or biweekly 50-minute sessions using mediated learning principles to work directly on identified cognitive patterns. The therapist acts as a cognitive mediator, not a director.

4
Group Sessions (Optional)

Small group work that provides a relational context for applying cognitive insights. Groups are theme-based and limited to 6–8 participants.

5
Ongoing Reflection

We build in structured self-reflection practices between sessions — because insight that stays only in the therapy room doesn't transfer to life.

The Foundation

Where this approach comes from.

Philosophy of Mind

Bernard Lonergan

Lonergan's account of human cognition — structured around the four levels of experience, understanding, judgment, and decision — gives us a precise map of where thinking can go well or astray. Our therapeutic work navigates this map.

Learn more about our approach
Cognitive Science

Reuven Feuerstein

Feuerstein's Structural Cognitive Modifiability proves that cognition is not fixed. His Mediated Learning Experience framework shows us how a skilled practitioner can intervene in the gap between experience and response — creating conditions for genuine cognitive change.
